AI-Based Early Cancer Risk Detection System

Medical AI Preventive Medicine Research

Goal

Build a screening-focused expert system, developed with guidance from a faculty member at PSAU, to explore early cancer risk estimation using symptoms and basic clinical indicators.

Approach

Used data mining methods that combine classification, clustering, and decision-tree rules to support structured risk estimation.

Outcome

Produced a prototype model and logic workflow that can flag higher-risk cases for earlier follow-up and referral.

Reflection

This project strengthened my interest in preventive medicine and showed how early screening tools can reduce avoidable delays.

Project Summary

Many serious illnesses progress quietly in the early stages. This project explored how an intelligent system could help detect cancer risk earlier by analyzing symptoms and accessible risk factors before expensive testing. The system was designed to support screening for cancers such as breast, lung, stomach, oral, and cervical cancer.

Key Components
  • Symptom-driven input and structured questioning to reduce missing information
  • Risk estimation using classification and rule-based decision support
  • Grouping patterns with clustering to support clearer case profiles
  • Clear output that supports next steps, follow-up, and prevention guidance
Impact Focus
  • Supports early detection and prevention for higher-risk patients
  • Improves time efficiency compared with full clinical testing as a first step
  • Encourages earlier referrals and more informed health decisions

Turner Syndrome Project

Medical Genetics Research Communication

Goal

Explain Turner syndrome using a patient-centered case story and accurate medical evidence.

Approach

Researched trusted sources, organized clinical findings, and wrote clear explanations for a general audience.

Outcome

Created a structured presentation with definitions, complications, diagnosis, and treatment planning.

Reflection

This project connected genetics to long-term care and helped me understand the importance of monitoring over time.

Image: assets/Projects%20and%20Research/turner.png

Case Summary
  • Sara is a 10-year-old girl who has always been the shortest in her class.
  • She gets tired easily and has frequent ear infections.
  • Her doctor hears a heart murmur and notices slow growth.
  • A karyotype test shows 45,X, confirming Turner syndrome.
Clinical Features and Complications
  • Short stature and slow growth
  • Webbed neck, low-set ears, broad chest, swelling of hands and feet at birth
  • Delayed puberty, no periods, infertility
  • Heart conditions such as bicuspid aortic valve and coarctation of the aorta
  • Kidney differences, thyroid disease, frequent ear infections, and hearing loss
  • Long-term risks: high blood pressure, aortic enlargement, osteoporosis, diabetes, high cholesterol
Cause, Diagnosis, and Treatment
  • Cause: missing all or part of one X chromosome (commonly 45,X; sometimes mosaic patterns)
  • Diagnosis: prenatal ultrasound clues and testing; confirmed after birth by blood karyotype
  • Treatment: growth hormone, estrogen and later progesterone therapy, regular heart, kidney, thyroid, and hearing monitoring
  • Support: educational and advocacy resources from recognized organizations

  • Cleveland Clinic. Turner Syndrome. Cleveland Clinic Health Library.

Unsafe Patient Handoffs in Hospitals

Patient Safety Evidence-Based Writing ENG 112

Goal

Explain how unsafe handoffs contribute to preventable harm and describe practical solutions.

Approach

Reviewed published studies, summarized findings, and organized solutions into a clear framework.

Outcome

Produced a research paper and a presentation summary supporting structured handoff tools and training.

Reflection

This project showed me how strong systems and communication practices can directly protect patients.

Image: assets/Projects%20and%20Research/patient.png

Presentation Highlights
  • A handoff is the transfer of responsibility for a patient during shift changes or unit transfers.
  • Missing information can lead to delays, wrong decisions, and preventable harm.
  • Structured tools such as I-PASS can reduce errors and harmful events.
  • Common causes include workload pressure, lack of a standard format, limited training, and fragmented documentation.
  • Solutions include I-PASS or SBAR, training and simulation, digital templates, and stronger safety culture.
References

  • Müller et al. (2018). BMC Medical Education.
  • Starmer et al. (2014). New England Journal of Medicine.
  • Tam et al. (2018). PLOS ONE.

Conics Project (MAT 172)

Math Modeling Real Measurement Equations

Goal

Identify conic sections in everyday objects and connect measurements to correct equations.

Approach

Observed real objects, measured dimensions, and translated measurements into standard equation forms.

Outcome

Collected examples of circle, ellipse, parabola, line, and hyperbola with equation work and explanations.

Reflection

This project helped me connect abstract formulas to real shapes and real measurements.

Image: assets/Projects%20and%20Research/conic.png

Introduction

Conic sections appear in everyday life, not just in textbooks. Objects like bowls, glasses, containers, and tubes naturally form circles, ellipses, parabolas, and hyperbolas. This project helped me recognize these shapes and connect what I see to the equations I learn in class.

Examples I Found
  • Circle: bowl (top view)
  • Parabola: tray curve
  • Ellipse: tilted glass opening
  • Line: door frame edge
  • Hyperbola: bottle or tube sides
Equations

Circle

  • Equation form: x2 + y2 = r2
  • Use the radius you measured so the final equation matches your numbers

Ellipse

  • Equation form: x2/a2 + y2/b2 = 1
  • Use your measured major and minor axes to compute a and b
