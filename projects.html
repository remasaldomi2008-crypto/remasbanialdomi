Image: assets/Projects%20and%20Research/turner.png

Meet Sara: A Girl with Turner Syndrome
  • Sara is a 10 year old girl who has always been the shortest in her class
  • She gets tired easily and has frequent ear infections
  • Her doctor hears a heart murmur and worries about her slow growth
  • A karyotype test shows 45,X, which confirms Turner syndrome
Clinical Features and Complications
  • Short stature and slow growth
  • Webbed neck, low set ears, broad chest, swelling of hands and feet at birth
  • Delayed puberty, no periods, infertility
  • Heart problems such as bicuspid aortic valve and coarctation of the aorta
  • Kidney differences, thyroid disease, frequent ear infections and hearing loss
  • Long term: high blood pressure, aortic enlargement, osteoporosis, diabetes, high cholesterol
What Causes Turner Syndrome
  • Turner syndrome occurs when all or part of one X chromosome is missing
  • Most common pattern: 45,X (monosomy X)
  • Mosaic cases: 45,X / 46,XX
  • Can also involve structural changes in the X chromosome
  • Happens by chance during meiosis or early embryo cell division
  • Not caused by anything parents did
Diagnosis and Treatment
  • Diagnosis: ultrasound clues, CVS or amniocentesis, confirmed by karyotype blood test
  • Treatment: growth hormone, estrogen and later progesterone therapy, regular heart and hearing monitoring
  • Support: Turner Syndrome Foundation and Turner Syndrome Society of the United States

Unsafe Patient Handoffs in Hospitals

Patient Safety Evidence Based Writing ENG 112

Goal

Explain how unsafe handoffs cause preventable harm and propose solutions.

Approach

I researched published studies, summarized key findings, and organized solutions into a clear framework.

Outcome

A full research paper and a presentation summary focused on I PASS, SBAR, and training tools.

Reflection

This project showed me how communication systems can affect real patient outcomes.

Image: assets/Projects%20and%20Research/patient.png

Presentation Highlights
  • A handoff is the transfer of responsibility for a patient, like shift changes and unit transfers
  • Missing information can lead to delays, wrong decisions, and preventable harm
  • I PASS reduced errors and harmful events (Starmer et al., 2014)
  • Main causes include workload pressure, lack of a standard format, limited training, and fragmented documentation
  • Solutions include I PASS or SBAR, training and simulation, and digital templates
References
  • Abraham et al. (2013). Journal of the American Medical Informatics Association.
  • AHRQ (2019). SBAR technique for communication.
  • Manser and Foster (2020). International Journal for Quality in Health Care.
  • Müller et al. (2018). BMC Medical Education.
  • Starmer et al. (2014). New England Journal of Medicine.
  • Tam et al. (2018). PLOS ONE.

Conics Project (MAT 172)

Math Modeling Real Measurement Equations

Goal

Identify conic sections in everyday objects and connect measurements to correct equations.

Approach

I observed objects, measured dimensions, and derived the equations.

Outcome

Examples of circle, ellipse, parabola, line, and hyperbola with equation work.

Reflection

This helped me see math as something real, not just formulas.

Image: assets/Projects%20and%20Research/conic.png

Introduction

Conic sections appear everywhere in real life, not just in math class. Objects like bowls, glasses, containers, and tubes create circles, ellipses, parabolas, and hyperbolas. This project helped me recognize these shapes around my home and connect them to equations.

Examples I Found
  • Circle: bowl (top view)
  • Parabola: tray curve
  • Ellipse: tilted glass opening
  • Line: door frame edge
  • Hyperbola: bottle or tube sides
Equations

Circle

  • Equation form: x2 + y2 = r2
  • Use the radius you measured so the final equation matches your numbers

Ellipse

  • Equation form: x2/a2 + y2/b2 = 1
  • Use your measured major and minor axes to compute a and b
