Paper Shelter Challenge

I made a small cabin using only paper and glue. The cabin is white and brown, giving it a cozy and rustic look. I used folding and cutting techniques to create the walls and roof. The white color makes it look clean, while the brown gives it a warm, natural feel. The hardest part was making sure the paper stayed glued together, but I made sure everything was secure. The cabin looks simple, but it took a lot of care to put it together.

Color Wheel

In my Interior Design class, I created a color wheel and learned how colors work together in a space. I discovered the primary colors—red, blue, and yellow—and how they mix to make secondary colors like green, orange, and purple.

I also learned about hue, tint, tone, and shade. These helped me understand how designers use light and dark colors to create different moods in a room. This project was fun and helped me see how important color is in interior design.

Floorplan

In my Interior Design class, I created a floor plan where I designed two rooms. This project helped me understand how to plan spaces and think about how people move and live in a room. It was a fun way to practice layout and space design.

2- Point Perspective Bedroom

As part of my Interior Design class, I drew a bedroom using two-point perspective. This technique helped me make the room look more realistic by showing depth and angles. It was a great way to practice how to draw spaces that look 3D.

1- Point Perspective Name

In class, I practiced one-point perspective by writing my name using this drawing technique. It helped me learn how to show depth and make letters look like they are going back into space. It was a fun and creative way to understand perspective drawing.

Monochromatic Living Room

For one of my projects, I designed a living room using a monochromatic green color scheme. I used different shades, tints, and tones of green to create a calm and stylish space. This project taught me how using just one color in different ways can still make a room look interesting and balanced.

Monochromatic Bedroom

In this project, I designed a bedroom using a monochromatic yellow color scheme. I used light and dark shades of yellow to give the room warmth and brightness. This activity helped me see how one color can create a cozy and unified look when used in different ways.

Complementary Office

For my project, I designed an office using complementary colors—orange and blue. These two colors sit opposite each other on the color wheel, which makes the space lively and balanced. Using complementary colors helped me understand how contrast can make a room feel energetic and visually appealing.

2-Point Perspective boxes

In class, I practiced drawing boxes in two-point perspective. This helped me learn how to show depth and angles more realistically. It was a great exercise to improve my drawing skills and understand how objects look in 3D space.

Bubble Name

For this project, I made a bubble name artwork that included an abstract picture of a cat. I wrote my name in cursive in the middle of the design. This project let me be creative with shapes and colors while expressing my style in a fun and artistic way.

Foldable Bedroom

In this project, I created a foldable bedroom, where everything in the room can fold or collapse to save space. This design helped me think about smart, space-saving solutions, especially for small rooms. It was a fun and creative way to explore modern interior design ideas.

Foldable Living Room

For this project, I designed a foldable living room, where every piece of furniture can be folded or stored away to save space. This idea is perfect for small apartments or multi-use areas. I enjoyed thinking creatively about how to make the room both stylish and functional. It showed me how smart design can make everyday living more comfortable and efficient.

Foldable Kitchen

In this project, I created a foldable kitchen where all the elements—like the counters, table, and shelves—can be folded or tucked away to save space. This design is great for tiny homes or small apartments. It helped me think about how to make kitchens more flexible and efficient without losing style or function.

Foldable Bathroom

In this project, I created a foldable bathroom that includes a bathtub, mirror, table, and shelves—all designed to fold or move to save space. Even without a sink or toilet, the design focuses on comfort and smart storage. This project helped me think creatively about how to make a bathroom functional and stylish in a small space.

Design Thinking Poster

For this project, I created a poster about the steps of design thinking. I included all five steps: empathize, define, ideate, prototype, and test. On the poster, I explained what each step means and drew small pictures to show each one. This project helped me understand the creative process designers use to solve problems and come up with new ideas.

How to Make a Toast

For this project, I created a poster showing the steps to make toast. I included clear steps with pictures to make it easy to follow. This activity helped me think about how to break down simple tasks into clear instructions, just like in design thinking.

LOVE in 1-Point Perspective

In class, I practiced one-point perspective by drawing the word “Love.” This technique helped me learn how to add depth and dimension, making the letters look like they stretch back into space. It was a creative way to combine art and design skills.
